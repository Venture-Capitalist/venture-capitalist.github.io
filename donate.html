Why Funding Matters

High-impact engineering programs — especially FIRST Robotics — require a baseline of over $20,000 per year to cover registration fees, tools, machinery, parts, and travel. That barrier locks out the vast majority of students who don't attend a well-funded school or whose families can't cover costs independently.

Meanwhile, the US faces a serious shortage of engineering talent : there are roughly three open engineering jobs for every qualified candidate in 2026. Students who can't access hands-on STEM programs fall behind their peers in college admissions and career readiness.

ShadowCrystal exists to break this cycle. Your donation directly funds the parts, tools, registration fees, and facilities that enable students to expand their engineering skills.
